Dear Hiring Manager,

Great customer solutions engineering comes down to a repeatable process: identifying customer problems, building solutions, validating those solutions, and driving adoption across a diverse range of stakeholders. My work at Specialized is a strong example of what this looks like in practice.

In 2023, I joined the Test Lab, where new bike platforms undergo a rigorous validation process to ensure product safety and low warranty rates. Early on, I noticed engineers were spending up to a week manually compiling historical test and warranty data from scattered spreadsheets and reports. Recognizing this as a barrier to fast engineering decisions, I built a Python tool to retrieve, structure, and organize this data. What previously required days of manual work became a matter of hours.

This initiative led to a larger effort: migrating the lab’s legacy Jira-based test report database to a global Lab Information Management System (LIMS) built on the STARLIMS SaaS platform. This project required gathering requirements and user workflows across stakeholders in seven countries, including design engineering, testing, product development, supply chain, and external manufacturing partners.

My role was to translate these needs into functional requirements, configure and build system workflows aligned to each user group, validate the system through real user testing, and drive adoption across the organization. This meant bridging technical and non-technical stakeholders, aligning priorities, and ensuring the solution worked in real operational contexts—not just in theory.

Shortly after the project began, we learned the legacy system would be sunset by year-end, giving us a hard eight-month deadline. We successfully built, integrated, piloted, validated, and deployed a global system that not only replaced the legacy platform but significantly expanded its capabilities. These improvements enabled faster compliance reporting, tighter inventory control, and dramatically improved data accessibility.

At its core, Domo’s mission is to connect data to the people who need it—making it accessible, interpretable, and actionable across an organization. That is the same problem I solved at Specialized, turning fragmented, inaccessible data into tools that enabled faster and better decisions.

While I have not held a formal customer solutions engineering title, much of my experience has centered on the same core skills: understanding how organizations operate, identifying friction in complex systems, and guiding teams through adopting solutions that improve how they work. I’m excited to bring this systems-oriented, customer-focused approach to your engineering team.
